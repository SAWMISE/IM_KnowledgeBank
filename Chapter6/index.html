Evolution of the International Monetary System

Pre-1914

Gold Standard Era

Countries fixed their currencies to gold, providing stability but limited flexibility. UK led with £4.247 = 1oz gold, US at $20.67 = 1oz gold.

✓ Exchange Rate Stability ✓ Disciplined Monetary Policy ✗ Limited Crisis Response
1914-1944

Collapse & Instability

WWI and the Great Depression destroyed the gold standard. Countries abandoned fixed rates, leading to competitive devaluations and trade wars.

⚠ Currency Wars ⚠ Trade Barriers ⚠ Economic Chaos
1944-1971

Bretton Woods System

Dollar-based system with fixed exchange rates. Created the IMF and World Bank. US dollar pegged to gold at $35/oz, other currencies pegged to dollar.

✓ Dollar as Reserve Currency ✓ IMF & World Bank Created ✓ Stable Growth Period
1971-Present

Managed Float System

Flexible exchange rates with government intervention. G20 coordination, multiple reserve currencies (USD, EUR, GBP, JPY), ongoing evolution.

✓ Flexibility ✓ Crisis Adaptation ? Ongoing Reform

International Monetary Fund (IMF)

Promoting global monetary cooperation and financial stability

Primary Functions

  • Monetary Cooperation: Facilitate consultation on international monetary issues
  • Trade Growth: Support balanced expansion of international trade
  • Exchange Stability: Promote stable exchange rate arrangements
  • Payment Systems: Establish multilateral payment mechanisms
  • Crisis Support: Provide temporary financial assistance
  • Balance Correction: Help resolve payment imbalances

Special Drawing Rights (SDRs)

World Bank Group

Promoting economic development and poverty reduction worldwide

Five Main Institutions

IBRD

International Bank for Reconstruction and Development

Provides loans to middle-income countries for development projects

IDA

International Development Association

Interest-free loans and grants to the world's poorest countries

IFC

International Finance Corporation

Private sector investment facilitation in developing countries

MIGA

Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency

Political risk insurance and investment guarantees

ICSID

International Centre for Settlement of Investment Disputes

Arbitration and dispute resolution services

Case Study: China's Currency Policy

The Challenge

IMF concerns about China's undervalued renminbi affecting global trade balance

Business Impact

  • Companies competing with Chinese exports face unfair advantages
  • Foreign firms struggle to enter Chinese market
  • Global supply chains affected by currency manipulation

IMF Response

Ongoing pressure for currency reform and increased domestic consumption in China
