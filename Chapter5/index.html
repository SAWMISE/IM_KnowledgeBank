From GATT to WTO

1947

GATT Formation

23 founding countries created rules governing international trade

  • Focus on tariff reduction
  • Most-favored-nation (MFN) principle
  • Non-discrimination in trade
1986-1994

Uruguay Round

8-year negotiation period addressing complex trade issues

  • Expanded beyond goods to services
  • Addressed intellectual property
  • Led to WTO creation
1995

WTO Establishment

153 member nations with institutional framework

  • Dispute resolution mechanism
  • GATS (services) and TRIPS (IP rights)
  • Ongoing Doha Round (2001-present)

Current WTO Challenges

Agriculture

Export subsidies, import restrictions, and non-tariff barriers create ongoing disputes between nations.

Antidumping

Companies exporting below domestic prices or production costs face accusations and investigations.

Intellectual Property

Protecting creators' rights while facilitating global technology transfer remains complex.

Types of Economic Integration

1. Free Trade Area

Basic

Example: NAFTA

  • Remove internal trade barriers
  • Independent external trade policies
  • Origin-of-content rules apply

2. Customs Union

Intermediate

Example: Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC)

  • Common external trade policies
  • Unified tariff structures
  • Coordinated trade negotiations

3. Common Market

Advanced

Example: COMESA

  • Free movement of labor and capital
  • No work permits needed
  • Economic policy coordination

4. Economic Union

Complete

Example: European Union

  • Shared economic policies
  • Common currency (Euro)
  • Political integration elements

Case Study: Conflict Diamonds to Kimberley Process

The Problem

Angola's rebels sold $3.72 billion in "blood diamonds" to finance 27-year civil war, damaging the global diamond industry's reputation.

The Solution

Kimberley Process (2003): 74 governments adopted certification system ensuring 99%+ of diamonds are conflict-free.

Business Impact

Companies like De Beers restored reputation while promoting peace and ethical trade practices.

Business Impact & Strategy

Benefits

  • Trade Creation: More opportunities, cheaper prices for consumers
  • Employment Growth: Expanded job opportunities across member countries
  • Consensus Building: Easier agreements with smaller country groups
  • Consistent Criteria: Standardized investment and trade requirements
  • Reduced Barriers: Lower costs for cross-border operations

Challenges

  • Trade Diversion: May favor less efficient producers within bloc
  • Employment Shifts: Jobs move to cheaper labor markets
  • Sovereignty Loss: Nations give up policy-making rights
  • Complex Navigation: "Spaghetti bowl" of overlapping agreements
  • Rule Changes: Industry regulations change with new agreements

Strategic Business Response

Monitoring Systems

Companies maintain in-house teams to track WTO and regional trade developments

Industry Advocacy

Join organizations like US-ASEAN Business Council to influence trade policy

Multi-Regional Operations

Establish manufacturing in different blocs to access multiple markets

Legal Expertise

Combine business and legal education for trade career opportunities

Key Takeaways

1

Economic integration creates win-win scenarios that benefit all participants while promoting global peace and stability.

2

The evolution from GATT to WTO represents institutionalization of global trade governance with enforceable dispute resolution.

3

Regional blocs offer businesses expanded markets and reduced barriers, but require sophisticated navigation of complex agreements.

4

Peace and stability are fundamental prerequisites for optimal business operations and international trade growth.
